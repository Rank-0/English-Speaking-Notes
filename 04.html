English Speaking Note 4

क्या तुम्हें वाकई में लगता है कि तुम ये कर पाओगे?
(Kya tumhen vaakee mein (sach mein) lagta hai ki tum ye kar paoge?)
Do you really think, you will be able to do it?
Grammar Rule: Interrogative Sentence (Question) - Future Ability
Structure: Do/Does + Subject + really/honestly/truly/seriously + think + Subject + will be able to / could + Verb (base form) + Object?
Example Breakdown:
  • Do: Helping Verb
  • Subject: you
  • really: Adverb
  • think: Verb (base form)
  • you: Subject
  • will be able to: Future ability phrase
  • do: Verb (base form)
  • it: Object

यहाँ पर really की जगह honestly, truly, seriously भी बोल सकते हैं।

Do you honestly think, you will be able to do it?

Do you truly think, you will be able to do it?

Do you seriously think, you will be able to do it?

यहाँ पर कर पाओगे? के लिए will be able to की जगह could भी कह सकते हैं।

Do you really/honestly/truly think, you could do it?

Explanation: This sentence is a question asking if the listener genuinely believes they can accomplish something in the future. It uses modal verbs to express ability and possibility.

हाँ, बिल्कुल ! ये मेरे लिए बच्चों का खेल है।
(Haan, bilkul! ye mere liye bachhon ka khel hai.)
Yes, definitely! It’s a child’s play for me.
Grammar Rule: Affirmative Sentence - Expressing Ease
Structure: Yes, Adverb! It's + a/an + Noun phrase + for me.
Example Breakdown:
  • Yes: Affirmation
  • definitely: Adverb
  • It's: Contraction of "It is"
  • a child's play: Idiomatic noun phrase
  • for me: Prepositional phrase

आप definitely की जगह of course भी बोल सकते हैं।

Yes, of course! It’s a child’s play for me.

आप child’s play की जगह piece of cake या nothing भी कह सकते हैं।

Yes, of course! It’s a piece of cake for me.

Yes, of course! It’s nothing for me.

Explanation: This sentence expresses strong confidence and ease in performing a task. It uses idiomatic expressions to convey that the task is very simple.

मुझे लगता है कि तुम मेरे साथ मज़ाक कर रहे हो।
(Mujhe lagta hai ki tum mere saath mazaak kar rahe ho.)
I think you are kidding me.
Grammar Rule: Present Continuous Tense - Expressing Suspicion
Structure: Subject + think + (that) + Subject + are + Verb (ing form) + Object.
Example Breakdown:
  • Subject: I
  • think: Verb (base form)
  • that: Conjunction (optional)
  • Subject: you
  • are: Helping verb
  • kidding: Verb (ing form)
  • me: Object

कि के लिए यहाँ में that का यूज़ कर सकते हैं।

I think that you are kidding me.

Explanation: This sentence expresses the speaker's belief that the listener is joking or not being serious.

बिल्कुल नहीं ! ये वाकई में मेरे लिए बच्चों के खेल से ज़्यादा कुछ नहीं है।
(Bilkul nahin ! ye vaakee mein mere liye bachhon ke khel se zyada kuchh nahin hai.)
Not at all! it’s really not more than a child’s play for me.
Grammar Rule: Negative Affirmation - Expressing Extreme Ease
Structure: Not at all! It's + Adverb + not more than + a child's play + for me.
Example Breakdown:
  • Not at all: Negative affirmation
  • It's: Contraction of "It is"
  • really: Adverb
  • not more than: Comparative phrase
  • a child's play: Idiomatic noun phrase
  • for me: Prepositional phrase

Explanation: This sentence strongly denies the previous statement and emphasizes that the task is extremely easy.

देखते हैं कि तुम कर पाते हो या नहीं।
(Dekhate hain ki tum kar paate ho ya nahin.)
Let’s see whether you could do it or not.
Grammar Rule: Imperative Sentence - Expressing Doubt
Structure: Let's see + whether + Subject + could + Verb (base form) + it + or not.
Example Breakdown:
  • Let's see: Imperative phrase
  • whether: Conjunction
  • Subject: you
  • could: Modal verb
  • do: Verb (base form)
  • it: Object
  • or not: Alternative phrase

जब भी “कि…या” का Combination होता है तो “whether…or” का यूज़ किया जाता है।

कर पाना के लिए हम Could का यूज़ करते हैं, जैसा हमने इस sentence में किया।

Explanation: This sentence expresses doubt about the listener's ability and proposes to observe their attempt.

क्यों? क्या हुआ? तुम्हें मुझ पे विश्वास नहीं है क्या?
(Kyon? kya hua? tumhen mujh pe vishvaas nahin hai kya?)
Why? What happened? Do you not trust me?
Grammar Rule: Interrogative Sentence - Expressing Doubt
Structure: Why? What happened? Do + Subject + not + Verb (base form) + Object?
Example Breakdown:
  • Why: Interrogative adverb
  • What happened: Interrogative phrase
  • Do: Helping verb
  • Subject: you
  • not: Negative particle
  • trust: Verb (base form)
  • me: Object

चूँकि interrogative sentence है तो Helping Verb Do के साथ not को शुरुआत में मिला सकते हैं और आप कह सकते हैं – Don’t you trust me?

Explanation: This sentence questions the listener's doubt and seeks reassurance.

नहीं, ऐसा नहीं हैं; पर मैंने आज तक ऐसा एक भी व्यक्ति नहीं देखा है जो ये कर पाये।
(Nahin, aisa nahin hain; par mene aaj tak aisa ek bhee vyakti nahi dekha jo ye kar )
Nothing like that but I haven’t seen even a single person in my life, who could do it.
Grammar Rule: Negative Affirmation - Expressing Skepticism
Structure: Nothing like that but Subject + haven't seen + even a single person + in my life + who + could + Verb (base form) + it.
Example Breakdown:
  • Nothing like that: Negative affirmation
  • but: Conjunction
  • Subject: I
  • haven't seen: Present perfect negative
  • even a single person: Noun phrase
  • in my life: Prepositional phrase
  • who: Relative pronoun
  • could: Modal verb
  • do: Verb (base form)
  • it: Object

Explanation: This sentence clarifies that there is no personal distrust but expresses skepticism based on past experience.

लेकिन आज तुम ऐसा व्यक्ति देखोगे।
(Lekin aaj tum aisa vyakti dekhoge.)
But today, you will see such a person.
Grammar Rule: Simple Future Tense - Expressing Confidence
Structure: But + today, Subject + will + Verb (base form) + Object.
Example Breakdown:
  • But: Conjunction
  • today: Adverb
  • Subject: you
  • will: Helping verb
  • see: Verb (base form)
  • such a person: Object

Explanation: This sentence expresses the speaker's confidence that they will prove their ability.

अच्छा ठीक है, अगर तुम ये कर दोगे, तो मैं तुम्हें रू 5000 दूँगा।
(Acha theek hai, agar tum ye kar doge, to main tumhen rup 5000 doonga.)
Ok fine; if you do it, I’ll give you Rs 5000.
Grammar Rule: Conditional Sentence (Type 1) - Offering a Reward
Structure: Ok fine; if + Subject + Verb (simple present), Subject + will + Verb (base form) + Object.
Example Breakdown:
  • Ok fine: Interjection
  • if: Conjunction
  • Subject: you
  • do: Verb (simple present)
  • it: Object
  • Subject: I
  • will give: Verb (simple future)
  • you: Indirect object
  • Rs 5000: Direct object

Explanation: This sentence sets a condition for a reward, expressing a willingness to pay if the task is completed.

इतने बड़े रिस्क के लिए केवल रू 5000! कम से कम रू 10000 तो ऑफर करो।
(Itne bade risk ke liye keval rup 5000! kam se kam rup 10000 toh offer karo.)
Only Rs. 5000 for such a huge risk! At least offer Rs.10000.
Grammar Rule: Exclamatory Sentence - Expressing Disappointment and Request
Structure: Only + Amount + for + such a + Adjective + Noun! At least + Verb (base form) + Amount.
Example Breakdown:
  • Only Rs. 5000: Exclamation
  • for: Preposition
  • such a huge risk: Noun phrase
  • At least: Adverbial phrase
  • offer: Verb (base form)
  • Rs.10000: Amount

Explanation: This sentence expresses disappointment at the offered reward and requests a higher amount.

ठीक है, मैं तुम्हें रू 10000 दूँगा।
(Theek hai, main tumhen rup 10000 doonga.)
Ok, I’ll give you Rs.10000.
Grammar Rule: Simple Future Tense - Agreeing to a Request
Structure: Ok, Subject + will + Verb (base form) + Object.
Example Breakdown:
  • Ok: Interjection
  • Subject: I
  • will give: Verb (simple future)
  • you: Indirect object
  • Rs.10000: Direct object

I’ll (आइल) – I will

Explanation: This sentence confirms the agreement to pay the requested amount.

पैसे तैयार रखना !, तुम हारने वाले हो।
(Paise taiyaar rakhna ! tum harne wale ho.)
Keep the money ready. You are gonna lose.
Grammar Rule: Imperative and Future Continuous - Expressing Confidence
Structure: Verb (base form) + Object! Subject + are + gonna + Verb (base form).
Example Breakdown:
  • Keep: Verb (imperative)
  • the money ready: Object
  • Subject: You
  • are gonna lose: Future continuous (colloquial)

gonna = going to

Explanation: This sentence expresses the speaker's confidence in winning and warns the listener to be prepared to pay.

देखते हैं, समय बतायेगा।
(Dekhate hain, samay batayega.)
Let’s see, time will tell.
Grammar Rule: Simple Future Tense - Expressing Uncertainty
Structure: Let's see, Subject + will + Verb (base form).
Example Breakdown:
  • Let's see: Imperative phrase
  • Subject: time
  • will tell: Verb (simple future)

Explanation: This sentence suggests that the outcome is uncertain and will be revealed in the future.
